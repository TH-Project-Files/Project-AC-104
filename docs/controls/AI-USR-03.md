# AI-USR-03: Advanced AI Phishing Simulations: Update Security Awareness Training to include simulations specifically mimicking high-quality, multilingual LLM-generated phishing models.

**Category:** Governance & People (Layer 0: User Facing Controls)  
**Implementation Group:** IG 1  
**Aggregate Risk Level:** 2-Medium  
**CIS v8 Safeguards:** 14.1, 14.2  
**NIST CSF Subcategories:** PR.AT

---

## Details
Detailed Description:
This recommendation involves updating security awareness programs to use AI-generated phishing simulations that mimic the high-quality, multilingual, and personalized attacks created by large language models (LLMs).

Why AI Compounds Risk:
AI exacerbates phishing risks by enabling attackers to automate the creation of highly convincing, error-free messages at scale, bypassing traditional red flags like poor grammar while tailoring content to specific roles, industries, and languages.

Examples:
1. Deploy role-specific spear-phishing simulations that use AI to gather employee context, such as job titles and common tools, to generate realistic internal or vendor-related lures.
2. Integrate multilingual simulations into global training programs to test employees in their native languages using AI-cloned voices or translated text that maintains natural tone and nuance.
3. Use AI-driven platforms to provide automated remediation training, where employees who fail a simulation are immediately assigned personalized learning modules based on the specific type of attack they encountered.
