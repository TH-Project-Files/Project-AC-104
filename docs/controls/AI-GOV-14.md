# AI-GOV-14: Implement Cryptographic Provenance (e.g., C2PA standards) for all official executive and corporate media to establish verifiable digital trust.

**Category:** Governance & People (Layer 0: Governance & System Controls)  
**Implementation Group:** IG 3  
**Aggregate Risk Level:** 3-Low  
**CIS v8 Safeguards:** 14.1, 17.1  
**NIST CSF Subcategories:** PR.AT, RS.MA

---

## Details
Detailed Description:
This recommendation involves attaching a tamper-evident, cryptographically signed digital record called a C2PA Manifest to media files like photos and videos. This manifest acts as a digital nutrition label that records the content's origin, the tools used for creation, and any subsequent edits, allowing anyone to verify the asset's history and authenticity.

Why AI Compounds Risk:
AI exacerbates the risk of misinformation because generative AI can create hyper-realistic deepfakes and synthetic media that are visually indistinguishable from authentic content. This erosion of trust makes it difficult for audiences to discern reality from manipulation, leading to potential harm, fraud, and the degradation of public discourse.

Examples:
1. Integration with creative workflows by using C2PA-enabled professional creative software or AI media generation services to automatically embed provenance data into assets during the production and export process.
2. Deployment of hardware-based trust by equipping executive communication teams with C2PA-compliant cameras and smartphones that sign and hash media at the point of capture.
3. Implementation of secure verification portals or browser extensions that allow stakeholders and the public to inspect the Content Credentials of official corporate media to confirm it has not been tampered with.
